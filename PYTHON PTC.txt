        ASSIGNMENT OPERATORS

        =  : simple assignment
        =  : add and assign (e.g., `x += 1`)
        -=  : subtract and assign
        *=  : multiply and assign
        /=  : divide and assign
        //= : floor divide and assign
        %=  : modulus and assign
        **= : exponent and assign
        <<= : left shift and assign
        >>= : right shift and assign
        &=  : bitwise AND and assign
        |=  : bitwise OR and assign
        ^=  : bitwise XOR and assign
        :=  : assignment expression (walrus operator)

        OPERATOR PRECEDENCE (high → low)

        `()` : parentheses (grouping), function calls, indexing, slicing
        `**` : exponentiation (right-associative)
        Unary: `+x`, `-x`, `~x`
        `*`, `/`, `//`, `%` : multiplication, division, floor division, modulus
        `+`, `-` : addition, subtraction
        `<<`, `>>` : bitwise shifts
        `&` : bitwise AND
        `^` : bitwise XOR
        `|` : bitwise OR
        Comparisons: `in`, `not in`, `is`, `is not`, `<`, `<=`, `>`, `>=`, `!=`, `==`
        `not` : boolean NOT
        `and` : boolean AND
        `or` : boolean OR
        Conditional expression: `x if cond else y`
        `lambda` : lambda expression (lowest precedence)

        Note: `=` is a statement and not an expression; it doesn't participate in expression precedence.
